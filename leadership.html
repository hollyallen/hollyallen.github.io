Leadership Resources

Here are some writings about leadership, especially leading software engineers, that have resonated for me over the years.

As a manager and leader, some of the most important work you can do is to actively work against unconcious bias, and promote diversity and inclusion in your workplace.

  • Project Include is an open community working toward providing meaningful diversity and inclusion solutions for tech companies.
  • Google's re:Work is a collection of practices, research, and ideas from Google to "put people first". Most resonate with me. Please especially read their writing about unconcious bias.
  • Facebook's guide to managing bias
  • The 101-Level Reader: Books to help you better understand your biases and the lived experiences of people from Ashe Dryden
  • Feedback guidance by me
  • One on Ones Guide by my team and I
  • Being Awesome by Kate Matsura
  • Leadership by Kate Matsura
  • Rands in Repose blog. I haven't read his books, so I can't recommend them personally, but I hear good things.
  • Turn the Ship Around by David Marquet. Okay, I haven't read this book, but everything I've heard points to it being great.
  • The Energy Project is a blog, a newsletter, and the founder Tony Schwartz often writes op eds in the New York Times. Their key ideas about creativity and energy renewal really resonate with me.
  • Marc Esher has a great list of resources too.
